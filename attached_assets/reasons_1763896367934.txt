# Google Play Store App Rejection - Common Reasons

Google Play Store app rejections happen frequently. In 2023, Google blocked 2.28 million policy-violating apps. To ensure your B10 News app doesn't get rejected, you need to understand these common rejection reasons.

## 1. Privacy & Data Safety Policy Violations

**Privacy Policy Issues:**
- **Missing Privacy Policy**: No privacy policy link or the link not working
- **Invalid URL**: PDF files, editable documents, 404 errors - these are not accepted
- **Incomplete Policy**: Not mentioning what data you collect and whether you share it with third parties
- **Missing Permissions Explanation**: Not explaining in the privacy policy what permissions the app requests
- **No Data Deletion Method**: Not explaining how users can delete their data

**Data Safety Form Errors:**
- Data Safety section is incomplete
- Not truthfully reporting the data you collect
- Not mentioning third-party SDKs
- Missing user consent mechanisms

**Privacy Policy Requirements:**
- Developer name and contact information
- Collected data types
- Data usage purposes
- Third-party data sharing
- Data security measures
- Data retention policy
- User data deletion process

## 2. Metadata Policy Violations

**Keyword Spam:**
Google strictly enforces metadata spam violations. Rejected keywords include:

*Banned words in App Title/Short Description:*
- "Best", "#1", "Top", "New"
- "Free", "No Ads", "Ad-Free"
- "Download Now", "Install Now", "Update Now"
- "Million Downloads", "Trending"
- "Sale", "Discount", "Bonus"

**Additional Violations:**
- **Repetitive/unrelated keywords**: Writing the same words multiple times
- **ALL CAPS text**: Using ALL CAPS outside of brand names
- **Emojis in title/developer name**: Putting emojis in app icon or title
- **Repeated special characters**: Using !!!, ???, ***
- **Misleading descriptions**: Promising features that don't exist in the app
- **Exaggerated claims**: Using words like "World's best", "Perfect"

**Inaccurate Metadata:**
- **Screenshots/videos misleading**: Showing features in screenshots that don't exist in the app
- **False advertising**: Using unrealistic promo materials
- **Incorrect category**: Selecting the wrong category
- **Placeholder content**: Leaving "Lorem ipsum", test text

## 3. Sensitive Permissions Issues

**Unnecessary Permissions Requests:**
Google rejects apps that request excessive permissions:
- Camera, Location, Contacts - without clear justification
- SMS, Call logs - when not core functionality
- Storage access - when accessing unnecessary files
- Microphone - when audio recording isn't necessary

**Permission Declaration Issues:**
- Even if you declare core functionality in the Permission Declaration Form, the app will be rejected if actual usage is different
- Not providing users with a "why" explanation when requesting permissions
- Permissions not matching app functionality

**Solution:**
- Request only necessary permissions
- Ask for permissions in context - when the user is using that feature
- Clearly explain the rationale for permissions
- Accurately declare permission use cases in Play Console

## 4. Restricted & Inappropriate Content

**Prohibited Content:**
- Violence or terrorism: Graphic violent content
- Explicit material: Adult/pornographic content
- Dangerous content: Content harmful to children
- Gambling: Real money gambling, games
- Hate speech: Discrimination, harassment
- AI-generated deepfakes: Misleading AI content
- Drugs/illegal activities: Promotion of illegal substances

**User-Generated Content Issues:**
- Lack of proper moderation for user-generated content
- No mechanism to remove offensive comments/posts

**Content Rating Problems:**
- App content not matching declared rating
- Declaring "Everyone" rating when mature content exists

## 5. Copyright & Intellectual Property Policies

**Copyright Infringement:**
- **Cloning popular apps**: Copying famous apps
- **Unlicensed content**: Music, images, videos without permission
- **Trademark violations**: Unauthorized usage of brand names, logos
- **Code copying**: Duplicating other apps' functionality
- **Impersonation**: Pretending to be other brands/apps

**News Apps Specific (Important for your B10 News app):**
- Give credit to original sources when publishing news articles
- If using third-party copyrighted images, verify proper attribution/license
- Don't copy-paste content from other news sources

## 6. Technical Problems & Performance Issues

**App Crashes & Bugs:**
- **Frequent crashes**: App crashing when opening
- **Force closes**: Sudden shutdowns
- **Freezing**: App not responding
- **Load failures**: App not loading even after installation
- **Broken features**: Core features not working
- **Poor performance**: Slow, laggy experience
- **Not responsive**: Buttons, navigation not working

**Incomplete Apps:**
- **Beta versions**: Having "Beta" label in production
- **Placeholder content**: "Coming soon", dummy text
- **Missing features**: Not implementing advertised features
- **Unfinished builds**: Submitting half-complete app

## 7. Minimum Functionality Policy Violations

**Limited Functionality:**
Since August 2024, Google enforces a "Minimum Functionality" policy:
- **Static content only**: Text-only or PDF-only apps
- **Single wallpaper apps**: Very limited content
- **No engaging features**: Apps with no user interaction
- **Basic web wrapper**: Simply converting a website into an app

**Solution:**
- Add interactive features (comments, ratings, sharing)
- Implement user engagement features
- Provide regular updates and fresh content

## 8. Malicious Behavior & Security Issues

**Suspicious Activity:**
- **Malware/spyware**: Malicious code
- **Trojans/viruses**: Security threats
- **Deceptive practices**: Cheating users
- **Unsafe links**: Redirects to phishing websites
- **Background data collection**: Secret data gathering
- **Unsecured data transmission**: Missing encryption

**Hidden Functionality:**
- Using permissions without mentioning in app description
- Including undisclosed features in the app

## 9. Improper Monetization

**Misleading In-App Purchases:**
- **Hidden fees**: Unexpected charges
- **Deceptive subscriptions**: Not clearly mentioning auto-renewal
- **Intrusive ads**: Ads disrupting user experience
- **Misleading ad placements**: Ads on YouTube videos (for news apps)
- **Unclear pricing**: Not clearly showing purchase amounts

**Ad Policy Violations:**
- Excessive ads affecting app usability
- Deceptive ad practices

## 10. Technical Requirements Violations

**Target API Level Issues:**
- **API 35 requirement**: From August 31, 2025, new apps must target Android 15 (API level 35)
- **Existing apps**: Minimum target should be Android 14 (API level 34)
- Outdated API levels will be rejected

**Device Compatibility Problems:**
- **Screen size issues**: Not displaying properly on different devices
- **Android version incompatibility**: Not supporting old/new Android versions
- **Hardware issues**: Crashes on specific devices

## 11. Copycat & Duplicate Apps

**Lack of Originality:**
- **Exact copies**: Duplicating existing apps
- **No unique value**: Apps with no differentiation
- **Same app multiple times**: Resubmitting with minor changes
- **Template apps**: Publishing unmodified template apps

## 12. Additional Common Issues

**Poor UI/UX Design:**
- Outdated, ugly interface design
- Confusing navigation
- Design guideline violations
- Inconsistent layout

**Incomplete Information:**
- Unclear app description
- Missing contact details
- No support information

**Legal Compliance:**
- Violations of local laws
- Non-compliance with regional regulations
- Missing legal disclaimers

## What to Do After Rejection?

**Review Process:**
1. **Email notification**: Google sends the exact rejection reason in an email
2. **Play Console check**: Detailed violation information is available
3. **Policy documentation**: Reference links are provided

**Fix & Resubmit:**
- Carefully read and understand the issue
- Fix the mentioned violations
- Review the related policies
- Resubmit the compliant version
- Prepare supporting documentation/screenshots if needed

**Appeal Process:**
- You can write well-reasoned appeals
- Support with screenshots, videos
- Provide clear explanation

**Important Notes:**
- **Previous version**: If an update is rejected, the old version remains live
- **Don't republish**: Don't resubmit the same app without fixing violations
- **Account strikes**: Multiple rejections can lead to account suspension

## Checklist to Avoid Rejection

**Pre-Submission:**
✅ Thoroughly test on real devices
✅ Use internal/closed testing tracks
✅ Run Pre-launch Report
✅ Integrate Firebase Crashlytics
✅ Review all policy checklists
✅ Fill Data Safety section accurately
✅ Privacy Policy should be working, complete, non-editable URL
✅ Request only necessary permissions
✅ Check metadata for spam
✅ Verify that screenshots accurately represent app functionality
✅ Declare content rating correctly
✅ Confirm you meet Target API level requirements

**Specifically for your B10 News app, focus on:**
- News content copyright
- Privacy policy (user data collection)
- Metadata (avoid words like "Best news" in title)
- Proper permissions justification

By avoiding these common rejection reasons, your app has a higher chance of being approved on the first attempt.
